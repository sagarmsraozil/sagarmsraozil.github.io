EWarehouse (Named as Nexus): (February,2025 - January,2026)

I partnered with a friend of mine for the startup to solve the headaches of managing the mess of the warehouse digitally, to track the items, and drop replenishment tasks. The project was introduced to bring the solution for the problems that we faced while working on the project to sell anime characters printed tshirts, and hoodies.

Problems:
1. Lots of stocks were missing due to irregular tracking.
2. Not a proper setup for the handshaking process between the sellers and the service provider.
3. Irregular supply of the stocks to the retail system. (Meaning the planning to sell the stocks with lot based segregation was not visible).
4. Chaos market setup (No proper planning for pricing strategies)
5. Inventory in and inventory out for the stocks were done randomly. (Meaning if we get orders, we are just picking it up, and sending it to the customer. No idea which stock was just replenished and sent to the customer).
6. No proper setup for handling “Return to order” case (Even spreadsheet level data recording was not available)
7. Random replenishment of the drops (Meaning anything was kept anywhere, and different products were mixed up in the same area, it used to be like spaghetti of products).
8. Time consuming when there used to be a new order from the customer, because of improper management of stocks.
9. No proper idea of how many stocks are available to be pipelined to the market.
10. No state based calculations for the overall marshalling phase of the ordered item.

Approached solutions:
1. Build a hierarchical digital warehouse to keep the goods with the proper labelings, to enhance the searching of the goods, and proper management.
2. Build a workflow to register a new drop of the product, it works like a state machine. Starts from company registration and handshaking to product introduction, and its negotiations, and finally how the deal to bring the lots of product is fulfilled.
3. Build a replenishment system to handle incoming and outgoing of the stocks in a managed way.
4. Build a flow to create droplets for the drops of products.
5. Build a flow to create an inventory of items with respect to lots, and its pricing strategy.
6. Build a flow to create a market and pipeline the updated information about the product/variant to the retail market.
7. Build an addon system to handle sales for the specific products. (It works like if we want to add ½ price for any product in the inventory, we have to create a record from our addon).
8. Build a marshalling flow from getting a list of orders from retail in a certain time interval, and fulfilling it. (All the flows are saved according to the state, and the reached data points)
9. Build a system to support “Return to Order” flow, when customers ask for replacement or returns.


Multi tenant based clothing Ecommerce (Named as Aroma): (March, 2024 - January,2026)

During the time of building the digital warehouse system, we planned to build a system from which sellers can sell their products. It was a solution from which the subscribed customers from EWarehouse can take the feature of Aroma to sell their products as well.

Problems:
1. Clothing business owners don't have a proper platform to showcase their products. Available online platforms are focused on selling all kinds of items, from grocery to vehicles, and medicines including clothes.
2. Totally rely on social media marketplace, and with marketplace customers have trust issues, as they don’t know from where, and from whom they are purchasing the goods.
3. Not a proper detailing of the proposed product, it’s just some images of the product with a touch of social media ads. Possible buyers are forced to put their friction by sending messages to the seller group and ask each and everything, it consumes a lot of time.
4. Not a proper distribution of pricing, for the same product customer A is paying Rs 800, and customer B is paying Rs 900, and so on. It’s totally inconsistent.
5. Most of the companies are working with inconsistent catalog, as there is improper messaging regarding the availability of the product.
6. No proper messaging about what is happening for the product which customers have already paid for. They are already the owner of the product, they should have the privilege to go through the status of what’s happening to their product.
7. Messaging to buy an item doesn’t feel like shopping.
8. For the retail businesses, products are not registered digitally, and don't have a proper architectured flow of which products are getting sold, the business only runs on trust, if the proper data metrics are not available, we cannot predict what we should restock for the market.

Proposed solutions:
1.Create a search engine and search results page named as catalog, to showcase the products which are available for sales.
2. Create a feature where customers can keep their wishlisted item on the cart.
3. Create a feature where customers can reserve the selected cart items, and proceed to checkout.
4. Create a feature where customers can finalize their selected cart items, and make orders.
5. Create a feature where customers can check the history of their orders.
